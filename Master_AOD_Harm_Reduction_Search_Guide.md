## 1. Introduction: Why this guide exists

Google dorking (advanced search operators) helps AOD workers cut through noise and find the *actual document*, *original dataset*, or *current guidance*‚Äîespecially when things are buried in PDFs, hidden in ‚Äúpublications‚Äù directories, or published without strong SEO.

**Why use Google Dorks?**
*   **Find government documents** before they are properly indexed or linked on homepages.
*   **Locate hard-to-find service directories** that aren't advertised.
*   **Track down research evidence** and grey literature efficiently.
*   **Monitor drug alerts** across jurisdictions in real-time.
*   **Discover unpublished reports**, submissions, and "buried" PDFs.

**This guide is NOT about hacking.**
We do not use search operators to find vulnerabilities, exposed credentials, private client records, or anything that could harm people or services. This guide is about **legitimate discovery of public information** that supports harm reduction work.

**Centering Peer and Lived Experience Perspectives:**
This guide is written with deep respect for peer workers and people with lived experience of drug use. Peer knowledge is expert knowledge. This guide helps you find:
*   **Peer-led research and evaluations** that center lived experience
*   **Peer workforce development** resources and frameworks
*   **"Nothing About Us Without Us"** - Policy work by and for people who use drugs
*   **Peer advocacy and campaign materials** that challenge stigma
*   **Peer supervision, support, and wellbeing** resources

## 2. Safety, Ethics, and Privacy Rules (Non-negotiables)

### 2.1 Do no harm with search
Use these practices when searching sensitive topics:
*   **Avoid searching for identifiable people** (names + ‚Äúoverdose‚Äù / ‚Äúpositive result‚Äù / ‚Äúincident‚Äù) unless it is clearly in the public interest and you have organisational approval.
*   **Do not seek leaked or exposed personal data.** If you accidentally find it, **stop**, record the URL privately per policy, and escalate via your organisation‚Äôs information security pathway.
*   **Prefer de-identified, aggregated sources** (public health alerts, reports, published datasets).

### 2.2 Trauma-informed and stigma-free search terms
Google indexes the language *used at the time*. You may need to use stigmatising terms to find older documents, but always prefer people-first language in your own documentation.
*   **Prefer:** `"people who use drugs"` over `"drug addicts"`
*   **Prefer:** `"overdose"` / `"poisoning"` over `"ODed"`
*   **Legacy Searching:** Include variants only when necessary to locate legacy documents:
    *   `("people who use drugs" OR "drug user" OR "injecting drug use" OR "IDU")`

### 2.3 Legal & Clinical Currency
*   **Respect Copyright:** content found via dorks is subject to the same copyright content found via normal search.
*   **Clinical Guidance is Time-Sensitive:** For anything clinical (withdrawal, overdose management, dosing), always check:
    *   **Publication date**
    *   **Issuing authority**
    *   **Jurisdiction**
    *   **Currency (last updated)**

## 3. Quick Start: The 90-Second Workflow

When you need *anything* fast:

1.  **Define the task** (alert / policy / service / evidence / training).
2.  **Choose the authority domain** first: `site:*.gov.au` or a known org/university.
3.  **Force document formats**: `filetype:pdf` (or `docx`, `pptx`, `xlsx`).
4.  **Use exact phrases** for program names: `"take-home naloxone"` `"needle syringe program"` `"drug checking"`
5.  **Add a date constraint**: `after:2023-01-01` (or a range `2020..2024`)
6.  **Exclude junk**: `-jobs -careers -news -opinion -pdfviewer`
7.  **Open 2‚Äì3 candidates**, cross-check dates, and capture citations.

## 4. Core Operators (The Essentials)

These are the operators you will use daily. Master these first.

### 4.1 Essential Search Operators

| Operator | Purpose | Example |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **`site:`** | Limit to a specific domain or TLD. | `site:*.gov.au "drug alert"`<br>`site:health.nsw.gov.au naloxone`<br>`site:*.edu.au harm reduction` |
| **`filetype:`** | Find specific document types (pdf, xlsx, pptx, docx). | `filetype:pdf "harm reduction"`<br>`filetype:xlsx "service directory"`<br>`filetype:docx policy submission` |
| **`" "`** | Exact phrase matching. Finds these words in this exact order. | `"drug checking trial"`<br>`"supervised injecting facility"`<br>`"peer-led harm reduction"` |
| **`OR`** | Boolean logic. Search for *either* term. Must be UPPERCASE. | `("pill testing" OR "drug checking")`<br>`naloxone OR narcan OR Nyxoid`<br>`festival OR "music event"` |
| **`-`** | Exclude terms/sites. Removes noise. | `"harm reduction" -jobs -careers`<br>`site:*.gov.au -site:news.gov.au`<br>`"drug policy" -news -opinion` |
| **`intitle:`** | Search only within the page title. | `intitle:"drug alert" site:*.gov.au`<br>`intitle:"health warning" opioid`<br>`intitle:"annual report" harm reduction` |
| **`inurl:`** | Search within the URL string itself. | `inurl:publications filetype:pdf`<br>`inurl:submissions harm reduction`<br>`inurl:aod-services` |
| **`intext:`** | Search within page body text only. | `intext:"peer worker" training`<br>`intext:"lived experience" supervision` |
| **`after:`** | Documents published after a date (YYYY-MM-DD). | `site:*.gov.au "overdose" after:2024-01-01`<br>`"drug alert" after:2025-01-01` |
| **`before:`** | Documents published before a date. | `"drug policy" before:2020-01-01`<br>`festival harm reduction before:2023` |
| **`..`** | Number range (dates, prices, measurements). | `"overdose" Australia 2020..2025`<br>`festival attendance 10000..50000` |
| **`*`** | Wildcard. Matches any word. | `"drug * service"` (finds: drug treatment service, drug checking service)<br>`"* harm reduction"` (finds: festival harm reduction, community harm reduction) |

## 5. Advanced Operators & Techniques

### 5.1 `allintitle:` and `allinurl:`
Force *multiple* words into the title or URL. Highly restrictive but high precision.
*   `allintitle: drug alert opioid`
*   `allinurl: publications naloxone pdf`

### 5.2 `AROUND(n)` proximity searching
Useful when documents don‚Äôt use your exact phrase but words appear near each other.
*   `"naloxone" AROUND(5) "program"` (Finds "program for the distribution of naloxone")
*   `"drug checking" AROUND(8) evaluation`

### 5.3 `intext:`
Search for words specifically in the body text (ignoring title/URL).
*   `intext:"peer worker" training`

### 5.4 `related:` and `cache:`
*   `related:ndarc.med.unsw.edu.au` -> Finds similar research websites.
*   `cache:health.gov.au/drug-checking` -> View Google's cached version if the page is down or changed (note: Google is phasing this feature out, but it still works in some regions/contexts).

### 5.5 ‚ÄúDirectory Mining‚Äù Patterns
When you suspect PDFs are sitting in a publications directory without a clear link.
*   `site:health.nsw.gov.au inurl:publications filetype:pdf naloxone`
*   `site:*.gov.au inurl:uploads filetype:pdf "harm reduction"`

## 8. Workflows & Pro Tips

### 8.1 The "Source Ladder" (Validity & Trust)
When assessing documents, prefer sources in this order:
1.  **Govt Health Authority** (`*.health.*.gov.au`, `aihw.gov.au`) - Official policy, alerts, guidelines
2.  **University / Research Institute** (`*.edu.au`, NDARC, Burnet) - Peer-reviewed evidence
3.  **Reputable Sector Org / Peak Body** (`*.org.au`, NUAA, AIVL) - Practice wisdom, lived experience
4.  **Everything else** (blogs, media) = **context only**, not primary evidence

**Always verify:**
*   Publication date / last updated
*   Author credentials and affiliations
*   Issuing authority
*   Jurisdiction (NSW guidance ‚â† WA guidance)

### 8.4 Quality Control & Junk Removal

*   Stigmatizing language that isn't critiqued

## 10. Troubleshooting: When Google Fails

### 10.1 Zero Results?
*   **Remove `filetype:`** first. The info might be on a webpage, not a PDF.
*   **Loosen `site:`** restrictions (e.g., change `site:health.nsw.gov.au` to `site:nsw.gov.au`).
*   **Swap Terminology:**
    *   ‚Äúharm minimisation‚Äù vs ‚Äúharm reduction‚Äù
    *   ‚Äúopioid pharmacotherapy‚Äù vs ‚ÄúOAT/OST‚Äù
    *   ‚Äúpublic health warning‚Äù vs ‚Äúdrug alert‚Äù
*   **Use `AROUND(n)`** for looser phrasing.

### 10.2 Too Many Results?
1.  Add `"exact phrases"`.
2.  Add `filetype:pdf`.
3.  Add `site:*.gov.au`.
4.  Add `after:YYYY-MM-DD`.
5.  Add exclusions (`-news -opinion`).

### 10.3 Filtering Out Stigmatizing Content

As a peer worker, you may want to avoid stigmatizing or harmful content:

**Exclude stigmatizing language (when appropriate):**
```
[your search] -"drug addict" -"drug abuse" -"substance abuse" -junkie
```

**Prioritize people-first language:**
```
"people who use drugs" OR "people who inject drugs" [your topic]
```

**Find peer-reviewed vs peer-led:**
```
# Peer-reviewed (academic):
site:*.edu.au [topic] filetype:pdf

# Peer-led (lived experience):
site:*.org.au "peer-led" [topic] filetype:pdf
```

**Note:** Sometimes you need to search using stigmatizing terms to find older documents or understand how language has changed. That's okay‚Äîjust be aware of the context.

### 10.4 Broken Link or Deleted Page?
*   **Google Cache:** `cache:http://example.com/old-policy.pdf`
*   **Internet Archive:** Copy the URL and paste it into [web.archive.org](https://web.archive.org).

### 11.1 Quick Reference Cheat Sheet

**The "Why This Operator?" Guide:**

| Operator | What It Does | When To Use It |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| `site:*.gov.au` | **The Truth Source.** Official policy, data, and alerts. | Finding authoritative guidance, policy, alerts. |
| `site:*.edu.au` | **The Evidence.** Research trials, papers, analysis. | Finding peer-reviewed research, evaluations. |
| `site:*.org.au` | **The Practice.** Peer guidelines, training, advocacy. | Finding practice tools, peer resources. |
| `filetype:pdf` | **The Document.** Skips news to find the actual report. | Getting the source document, not media coverage. |
| `intitle:"drug alert"` | **The Warning.** Finds official health alerts instantly. | Monitoring contaminated supply, overdose alerts. |
| `after:2024` | **The Now.** Ensures you aren't reading 2012 guidelines. | Finding current, not outdated, information. |
| `"exact phrase"` | **Precision.** Finds these exact words in this order. | When you know the exact term (e.g., program names). |
| `OR` | **Options.** Finds either term (must be UPPERCASE). | Covering terminology variations. |
| `-exclude` | **Noise Filter.** Removes junk from results. | Removing jobs, news, opinion pieces. |
| `AROUND(5)` | **Proximity.** Words near each other but not exact phrase. | When exact phrase doesn't exist in documents. |

*   **Share carefully** - Some findings may need careful handling before sharing

The goal is always to find information that helps people who use drugs, supports better services, enables advocacy, or holds systems accountable.
